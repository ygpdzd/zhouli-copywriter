# Zhouli Allusion Bank

Use this file when the user wants stronger "大周礼时代" flavor, screenshot-like groupchat writing, or a reply that should feel more cited and ceremonial.

Do not paste allusions mechanically. Pick one or two, then map them back to the concrete modern object: message, exam paper, takeout, KFC, stock loss, long text, group debate, late reply, game lobby, shared bill, or apology.

## Friendship And Mutual Help

- 管鲍之交: use for friends understanding each other's difficulty without forcing a harsh judgment.
- 高山流水: use for rare understanding between friends, especially when a small act shows real sympathy.
- 鸡黍之约: use for keeping appointments, replying, showing up, paying back small favors.
- 桃园结义: use playfully for shared meals, shared costs, or staying loyal in a group chat.

Modern mapping:

- paying for KFC, buying milk tea, saving a seat, joining a game lobby, helping with a small task.
- The joke works when the modern object remains visible: do not replace "KFC" with only "food"; do not replace "group chat" with only "the hall of friends".

## Reading, Learning, And Exams

- 荀子积跬步: use for accumulated study, exam preparation, long effort.
- 韦编三绝: use for repeated reading, revising, or grinding through hard material.
- 囊萤映雪: use for difficult study conditions, endurance, or student hardship.
- 旧卷新题: not a fixed allusion, but useful as a ritual frame for finishing one stage and entering another.

Modern mapping:

- exam bell, test paper, long article, dense documentation, homework, project handoff.
- For "long text is hard to read", use a soft admonition: a long road need not be crossed in one step; a thick book need not be swallowed at once.

## Debate And Speech

- 君子和而不同: use for disagreement without personal attack.
- 以理服人: use for calm argument and accepting better reasons.
- 讷于言而敏于行: use when someone speaks too much but acts little, or when a quiet action matters more than a long explanation.
- 礼失而求诸野: use lightly when a group unexpectedly behaves with courtesy.

Modern mapping:

- group chat debate, comment section argument, being mocked, replying to criticism.
- Avoid repeating insults. Convert them to dignified phrases such as `轻慢之语`, `讥讽之辞`, or `失礼之言`.

## Restraint And Desire

- 柳下惠坐怀不乱: use cautiously for restraint; do not overuse when the user wants light humor.
- 宋弘不弃糟糠: use for loyalty, boundaries, and refusing temptation.
- 君子好逑: use for acknowledging human preference while preserving measure.
- 克己复礼: use for resisting impulse, overspending, rage posting, or late-night indulgence.

Modern mapping:

- impulse buying, staying up late, chasing beauty, angry replies, risky spending.
- Keep the tone warm; this register should not become moral scolding.

## Money, Loss, And Risk

- 千金散尽还复来: use lightly for small losses and recovery, not as investment advice.
- 塞翁失马: use for temporary loss, uncertain outcomes, and risk education.
- 一饭千金: use for remembering kindness or a small favor with big meaning.
- 量入为出: use for budgeting, spending, stock losses, and restraint.

Modern mapping:

- losing 100 yuan in stocks, buying too much, shared bills, red packet etiquette.
- Add a practical moral: a small loss can become tuition for risk awareness.

## Food, Drink, And Shared Joy

- 分甘共苦: use for sharing food or bearing small costs together.
- 箪食瓢饮: use for simple food, modest pleasure, or playful poverty.
- 酒逢知己: use for social drinking, celebration, or friends eating together.
- 食不厌精 is easy to overdo; use only for joking about food quality.

Modern mapping:

- KFC, golden chicken, cola, milk tea, barbecue, late-night snacks.
- Keep brand or object names if the humor depends on them.

## Time, Fatigue, And Self-Care

- 日出而作，日入而息: use for sleep, routine, and not staying up.
- 闻鸡起舞: use for early effort or preparing before deadline.
- 苟日新，日日新，又日新: use for improvement, graduation, life after exams.
- 行百里者半九十: use for final-stage persistence.

Modern mapping:

- staying up, procrastinating, exam season, daily practice, weekly routine.

## Endings

Use endings flexibly. Do not force every reply to end in `合乎周礼`.

- Ritual revival: `看来礼又要在这里兴盛了。`
- Gentle sigh: `此事想来，实在令人感叹。`
- Humble close: `这不过是我的一点浅薄见知，还望诸位先生指教。`
- Soft admonition: `这其中的道理，还是值得细细想一想的。`
- Boundary: `若志向不同，也只好各自安坐，不必强求。`
- Classic punchline: `如此，方可称得上是合乎周礼。`
